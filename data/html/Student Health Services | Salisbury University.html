Student Health Services

Visit us with any illness, injury or health-related question, from athlete’s foot to the flu. At Student Health Services, we can provide allergy shots, immunizations, contraception services, STI screening, diagnostic lab tests, psychological counseling referrals and much more. Check out our free self-care center to receive self-care packets for ailments such as headaches and sore throats, and know that we are here to help you with any health concern – large or small.

Students may also send a secure, confidential message to one of our registered nurses through the Student Health Web Portal. Log in to the portal at: myhealth.salisbury.edu, using your GullNet username and password. Go to “Messages”, and select “New Message.”

Our Mission

Our dedicated staff strives to treat your individual needs and advocate for campus-wide and community wellness. We promote the success of all students by helping you to achieve optimal health based on individual needs and diverse characteristics. In an ambulatory setting, we strive to provide excellent clinical care and education. Our program is accredited by the Accreditation Association for Ambulatory Health Care, Inc.

Patient Visit Hours and Appointments

Regular Hours:

  • Monday, Tuesday, Thursday and Friday: 8:30 a.m.- 4:00 p.m.
  • Wednesdays: 9 a.m.- 4:00 p.m.
  • Administrative Office Hours – Monday-Friday: 8 a.m.- 4:30 p.m.

Summer Hours:

  • Monday-Friday: 8:30 a.m.-2 p.m.
  • Administrative Office Hours – Monday-Friday: 8 a.m. – 2:30 p.m.

All patient visits are by appointment. Schedule an appointment at any hour through our Student Health Web Portal or contact our office during our posted hours.

For medical emergencies, call an ambulance at 911 or call University Police at 410-543-6222.

Campus Health Alerts

  • Stay updated on information from the CDC and health organizations, such as flu prevention, Lyme disease prevention and the Zika virus.
  • In addition, learn about opioid abuse and the campus resources available for concerns regarding drug and alcohol use.
  • Important Health Alert Regarding Electronic Cigarettes

    Important Health Alert regarding Electronic Cigarettes
    September 20, 2019

    The American Medical Association (AMA) has released a statement as follows; “Americans should not use electronic cigarettes while health officials investigate cases of severe lung illnesses that may be linked to the devices.”

    Please see the links below for the full text from the AMA, as well as information from the CDC.

    Students needing assistance with quitting e-cigarettes, or smoking in general are encouraged to schedule an appointment with our office. We have also included information from the smoke free website and “I want to Quit Hotline”.

    Smoking helpline: 1-877-44U-QUIT (1-877-448-7848). Local and state quit lines: 1- 800-QUIT-NOW (1-800-784-8669)

     Facts about E-cigarettes:

    • Most contain nicotine which is an addictive drug.
    • Nicotine can harm the developing brain. The brain is still developing until about the age of 25.
    • Using nicotine may also increase the risk for future addiction to other drugs.
    • Scientists still do not know the long term health effects of e-cigarettes.
    • Some of the ingredients found in e-cigarettes could be harmful to the lungs.
    • Defective e-cigarette batteries have caused some fires and explosions.

    Remember, Salisbury University is a smoke free campus, and this includes e-cigarettes.

    • AMA: Do Not Use Electronic Cigarettes (https://www.empr.com)
       
    • Get the facts about electronic cigarettes, their health effects and the risks of using e-cigarettes (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention - cdc.gov)
