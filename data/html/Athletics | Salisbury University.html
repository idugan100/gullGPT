Athletics & National Champs!

The roar of the crowd, the ping of the bat, and the distinct swoosh of the ball hitting the net. These are just a few of the sounds that convey the excitement of Salisbury University athletics. Our 21 varsity programs compete at the NCAA Division III level and have earned 23 team and 24 individual national championships.

For the most in-depth information on statistics and schedules, visit www.suseagulls.com.

Scholar Athletes

The Sea Gulls soar in competition and in the classroom. SU has had 44 student-athletes named CSC Academic All-Americans and the current student-athlete population on campus routinely carries a higher grade-point average than their non-athlete counterparts. SU has ranked in the top four on the Conference All-Academic list in each of the past 10 years.

National Champions

We know a thing or two about national championships! Men's Lacrosse, Baseball, Women's Lacrosse, Field Hockey, Track & Field... come out and see the best in DIII sports!

Helping Hands

SU student-athletes are always lending a helping hand on campus and off, from youth camps, to Habitat for Humanity, to work with the local community, to volunteering during move-in days to help other students. Student-athletes also work together through Student-Athlete Advisory Committee and Student-Athlete Mentors.

  • 23 Team National Championships
  • 24 Individual National Championships
  • 44 CSC Academic All-Americans

Stay Informed

Fans keep up with Sea Gull athletics through the athletics website at www.suseagulls.com with more information than they would know what to do with – from stats and schedules to biographical information on coaches, staff and student-athletes. On social media, fans can monitor what’s going on by following @suseagulls on Facebook, Twitter and Instagram.

New Nests

Since 2015, the athletic facilities have seen a major upgrade, with new complexes for eight of the varsity programs and improvements to two other facilities. Sea Gull Stadium – home to men’s and women’s lacrosse, field hockey, and football – was completed in spring 2016 and is one of the top stadiums at the Division III level. New facilities recently have been completed for men’s and women’s tennis, softball, and baseball, with major upgrades to Sea Gull Soccer Stadium, giving the Sea Gulls some of the top outdoor athletic facilities in the nation.

Watch the Sea Gulls

Established in 2012, the Sea Gull Sports Network gives fans the opportunity to watch most Salisbury University home athletic events live. Through the network’s partnership with BlueFrame Technology, the live webcasts generally feature two announcers, multiple cameras and replays to give the home viewer the feel of being at the game. SGSN has also proven to be an invaluable learning experience for SU students, as all of the in-game positions are staffed by undergraduates.

Support Your Gulls

The University’s Maroon & Gold Club is an organization open to all who love the Sea Gulls. Show your support by joining this dedicated group and enjoy exclusive events and cool benefits.
