Center For Student Achievement

Be the best student you can be! The Center for Student Achievement (CSA) helps you maximize your academic potential to make the most of your SU degree.

Academic Support for You

In collaboration with Academic Affairs and Student Affairs, the CSA supports your learning, academic success and personal growth through an array of programs, including success coaching, academic workshops, Supplemental Instruction and tutoring that are designed to maximize your academic achievement. Our goals are to:

  • promote academic success through strategic use of resources
  • increase your ability to become an independent learner
  • partner with faculty to create a "seamless learning environment" (Kuh, 1996)
  • help you develop competencies that will directly enhance your success in the classroom
  • support initiatives to increase student retention and graduation rates

Meet our friendly and helpful staff who are here to help you.

Services

  • Success Coaching

    Success Coaching connects you with a trained academic success coach who will help you develop study skills and set academic goals. We offer the following services for coaching: general success coaching for all students, academic recovery, progress reports, and mid-semester grades.

  • Supplemental Instruction

    Supplemental Instruction is for classes that students have found especially difficult over the years. You can meet weekly with a student who has exceled at the class to get the inside track on success.

  • Workshops

    Workshops are held throughout the semester to give you help on such topics as study skills and time management – two of our most popular options!

  • Tutoring

    Tutoring services are available by appointment for an array of subjects in undergraduate courses. Schedule your appointment through our Tutoring Page.

  • Scholar Holler

    The Scholar Holler Award is a recognition program designed to highlight exceptional students for their achievements.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What are the CSA hours?
    • Monday - Friday: 8 a.m. - 4:30 p.m.
  • Do I need to make an appointment for tutoring?

    It is preferred that you schedule an appointment in advance through Navigate. This helps our tutors be better prepared to serve you. See the tutoring page for information.

  • Who are the tutors?

    The CSA's tutors are Salisbury University undergraduate students who have excelled in the courses and topics they offer. All of our tutors are faculty-recommended and have been trained in tutoring techniques to help you succeed!

  • If I need help with time management, study skills, etc. can the CSA help me?

    Absolutely. We offer workshops as requested by campus groups and one-on-one success coaching sessions with our trained academic success coaches. Workshops and success coaching both offer specific and specialized help with a variety of student success topics, such as study strategies, test taking strategies, time management skills, motivation, and note-taking.

  • How long are the workshops?

    Like success coaching meetings, workshops vary in length from 30 to 60 minutes. We are happy to come out to the residence halls to offer workshops as well. Ask your RA to set up a workshop for your floor!

  • How do I make an appointment?

    To make an appointment call 410-677-4865 or stop by our office in Guerrieri Academic Commons Room 270 during our office hours.

  • How much do your services cost?

    NOTHING! Our services are free to SU students.

  • If I have questions about advising where do I go?

    Make an appointment with your advisor on the Navigate App or drop in to the Academic Advising Center located on the 1st floor of Blackwell Hall. If you are still deciding on a major, visit the Career Services office located on the 2nd floor of GSU or visit the Academic Advising Center for more information on majors and minors.

  • Can I get help with my writing skills?

    Yes! The University Writing Center, down the hall in Guerrieri Academic Commons 260, can help you with all your writing needs. Call 410-543-6332 to set up an appointment!

  • May I use your computers?
    Yes! As long as they are not in use for training or tutoring sessions, they may be used by all students.
