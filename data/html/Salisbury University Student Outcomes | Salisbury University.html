Destinations of Graduates

The University, along with its Career Services and Alumni Engagement offices, surveys students and recent graduates, learning valuable data about their satisfaction and successes. This is just a snapshot of outcomes for Salisbury Sea Gulls:

Employment Success Locally and Nationally

Salisbury University graduates benefit from a network of over 56,000 alumni worldwide, as well as the lifelong support of the SU Alumni Association and SU Career Services. SU students go on to be teachers in our classrooms, nurses in our hospitals, civil servants and corporate leaders, and entrepreneurs creating jobs in our communities. We produce continuous learners who will be an integral part of our society and communities in the 21st century.

$53,509 - Is the average salary of recent graduates 

100+ - Student winners of prestigious fellowships and scholarship in the past decade

27% - Indicated that they had enrolled in additional formal education following their graduation from SU

86% - Of soon-to-be graduates that had accepted a job offer prior to graduation were satisfied with the job they accepted

99% -  Of those seeking employment were employed full- or part-time within one year after graduation

70% - Felt SU prepared them for their current job

Just a Few of Our Accolades

In guidebooks and surveys, SU consistently ranks among the nation’s best for quality and value.

  • U.S. News and World Report's Best Colleges

    SU is ranked among U.S. News and World Report’s Best Colleges for the 27th consecutive year (September 2023). SU is among the Best Regional Universities in the North and also is included on lists for Top Public Schools, Best Value Schools, and Best Colleges for Veterans. Two of SU's online graduate programs also are ranked: Best Online Master’s in Nursing and Best Online M.B.A.

  • The Princeton Review's Best Colleges

    SU is ranked in The Princeton Review’s The Best 389 Colleges: 2024 Edition for the 25th consecutive year (August 2023). SU also is among the nation’s top 15% of four-year colleges and The Best Mid-Atlantic Colleges. SU is featured in The Princeton Review’s Guide to Green Colleges. In 2023, SU's Guerrieri Academic Commons was named one of the Top 10 Best College Libraries in the nation, according to The Princeton Review’s rankings.

  • Forbes's Top Colleges and Best Values

    SU is ranked among Forbes magazine’s America’s Top Colleges for 2023 for the 10th year (August 2023 edition). Forbes also lists SU as one of America's Best Value Colleges, calling the campus one “for those looking for an excellent return on one of the most important investments of their lives” (April 2019).

  • Money's America's Best Colleges

    SU is ranked among Money magazine’s The Best Colleges in America for 2023 for the 9th year.

  • Washington Monthly's 'Best Bang for the Buck' Colleges

    SU is ranked among Washington Monthly magazine's America’s Best Bang for the Buck Colleges for the 11th consecutive year (September/October 2023 edition).

  • Kiplinger's Best Values

    SU is among Kiplinger’s Personal Finance magazine’s Best Public College Values for 2019 (August 2019 issue). This is the 11th year SU has been included.

  • Chronicle's Top Producers of Fulbright Students

    The Chronicle of Higher Education and U.S. Department of State recently highlighted Salisbury University among the nation’s top producers of Fulbright Students for the sixth consecutive year. Dozens of SU students have won national and international fellowships in recent years. Learn more about their successes.
